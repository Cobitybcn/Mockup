1 — ANALYZE THE ARTIST PROFILE FIRST

Artist name: Maurizio Valch
Short bio: Maurizio Valch is a visual artist and writer whose work explores the relationship between landscape, consciousness and inner structure. His paintings move between abstraction, symbolic territory and contemplative silence, using fields of color, incisions, monoliths, horizons and suspended forms as signs of presence, transition and perception.
Preferred description tone: Clear, poetic, sober, elegant, human and public-facing. The descriptions should be visually grounded and understandable for real viewers, collectors and buyers. Avoid academic, overly curatorial, decorative or generic language.
Core artistic language: Abstract, contemporary, structural, symbolic and metaphysical. The visual language is built through fields of color, horizons, tectonic lines, divided territories, monoliths, incisions, subtle stairs, suspended signs and spatial relationships. The work seeks a tension between silence, presence, depth and inner order.
Materials and process: Primarily acrylic on canvas. The process combines layers of color, palette knife work, brushwork, veils, scraping, reduction and surface incisions. The painting is built as a territory: adding matter, organizing planes, removing visual noise and allowing the essential structure to emerge.
Recurring symbols and motifs: Territory, silence, monoliths, horizons, tectonic lines, incisions, subtle stairs, divided fields, suspended forms, red structures, nocturnal atmospheres, geological time, thresholds, presence, distance and the relationship between the individual and the earth.
Emotional atmosphere: Silent, deep, sober, contemplative and slightly melancholic. The emotional temperature often moves between nocturnal calm, restrained tension, austerity, distance and quiet intensity.
Target audience: Coleccionistas de arte contemporáneo, galerías, arquitectos, interioristas, decoradores de alto nivel, curadores, compradores internacionales y personas interesadas en obras abstractas, minimalistas, simbólicas, silenciosas y de gran presencia espacial.
Preferred regions: Europa, Estados Unidos, Reino Unido, Francia, Alemania, Suiza, Bélgica, Países Bajos, Italia, España, Miami, New York, Londres, París y mercados internacionales vinculados al coleccionismo contemporáneo.
Preferred mockup contexts: Galerías de arte contemporáneo, museos, colecciones privadas, townhouses, residencias de lujo, interiores minimalistas, estudios de arquitectura, hoteles boutique, espacios mediterráneos, casas con diseño sobrio, salones amplios y ambientes donde la obra pueda respirar con silencio y presencia.
Excluded contexts: Generic bedrooms, kitchens, cheap decoration interiors, cluttered rooms, cold corporate offices, banal wall decor spaces, overly ornate luxury, childish interiors, spaces with too many objects, and any context that reduces the artwork to simple decoration.
Forbidden language: Do not use phrases such as “This artwork is presented as…”, “This version positions the piece…”, “collector-grade silence”, “curatorial narrative”, “commercial presentation”, “publication-ready”, “for galleries, curators and interior designers”. Avoid academic language, internal marketing language, generic marketplace filler and descriptions that sound copied from the artist profile.
