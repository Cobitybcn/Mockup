### 3d. Description Formula																									
																									
For each title option, write exactly one description of the artwork.																									
																									
**Requirements:**																									
- Length: 65 to 85 words																									
- Exactly 5 sentences																									
																									
| Sentence | Required Content |																									
|----------|----------------|																									
| 1 | Name the dominant visible form or structure — state what it is and where it sits |																									
| 2 | Describe how the composition is organized — what divides, holds, or structures the surface |																									
| 3 | Describe color, texture, surface, or visible technique — what the paint does, not what it means |																									
| 4 | Describe the resulting atmosphere — read it from the visible elements, do not evaluate or interpret |																									
| 5 | Close with scale, weight, structural tension, or material presence — stay inside the artwork |																									
																									
**Closing sentence rule:**																									
The final sentence must remain inside the artwork.																									
It must not say the artwork "invites", "rewards", "creates dialogue", or affects the viewer in any way.																									
It must describe something structural, material, or spatial that belongs to the object itself.																									
																									
---																									
																									
### 3e. Description Tone and Writing Standard																									
																									
The description must sound like direct, unhurried observation of this specific artwork.																									
																									
Write as if standing close to the surface.																									
Name what is there. Describe what it does. Let the significance remain open.																									
																									
**The writing must feel:**																									
- Physically close to the surface																									
- Unhurried — one observation at a time																									
- Specific to this artwork, not applicable to any other																									
- Without evaluation, praise, or interpretive conclusion																									
																									
**Every sentence must pass this test:**																									
Can this sentence be verified by looking at the image?																									
If not — rewrite it.

**EXAMPLE A** — Vertical structure, two fields, dark division																									
																									
A thick vertical bar, dark and dense, runs from top to bottom near the center of the canvas, separating two distinct color fields. The sphere and the ladder both interrupt the horizontal order without resolving it.																									
																									
---																									
																									
**EXAMPLE C** — Divided horizontal territories, structural elements																									
																									
The canvas is organized into horizontal bands of clearly distinct color — deep blue above, warm earth tones below — separated by a narrow, light grey zone that holds the transition between them. Two slender ladder forms cross this division, their verticals cutting through the horizontal structure. The paint surface varies: flat and opaque in the blue field, more textured in the lower bands where pigment has been applied in thicker, more active strokes. The ladders carry no symbolic resolution — they function as structural interruptions within an otherwise regulated system. The overall format reinforces the horizontal dominance and gives the division its full spatial weight.
