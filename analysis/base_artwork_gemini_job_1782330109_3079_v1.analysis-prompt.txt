The following artist profile variables provide background context only:

Artist statement: My work is built from visible structure, surface, color and spatial tension. Descriptions should begin with what can be seen in each artwork before moving toward atmosphere or interpretation. Use clear, sober and precise language. Avoid over-explaining the work psychologically. Interpretation should remain restrained and only appear when it is supported by visible elements.
Visual language: Abstract, contemporary, structural and symbolic. The visual language is built through color fields, divided territories, monolithic forms, horizons, incisions, ladders, suspended elements and spatial relationships. Emphasis should remain on composition, surface, material presence, depth, silence and visual order.
Recurring symbols / motifs: Territory, silence, monoliths, horizons, tectonic lines, incisions, subtle stairs, divided fields, suspended forms, red structures, thresholds, distance, presence and the relationship between figure, space and surface. Preferred atmospheres: Silent, sober, restrained, contemplative and slightly melancholic. The atmosphere should be read from visible color, structure, surface and space. Avoid turning every artwork into a psychological or mystical reading unless the image clearly supports it.

Rules for using the artist profile:

* The artist profile may influence emotional temperature, silence, light, spatial mood, or atmosphere.

## SECTION 3 — TITLES, SUBTITLES, AND DESCRIPTIONS

Generate exactly 3 title options.

Each option must include:

* title
* subtitle
* description

### 3a. Title Distribution Rule

The three titles must anchor to different visible families:

Title 1 — dominant form or focal structure
Title 2 — color field, material surface, texture, pigment behavior, visible technique
Title 3 — structural division, edge, threshold, spatial tension, scale, format, or compositional pressure

Do not describe the same visual event using synonyms.

### 3b. Title Formula

Each title = visible anchor + controlled poetic or spatial resonance.

Requirements:

* Exactly 3 titles.
* Each title: 2 to 5 words.
* Natural English.
* No CamelCase.
* No hashtags.
* No SEO phrases.
* No filename language.
* No generic luxury-art language.

Preferred vocabulary, only when visibly supported:
field / center / recess / surface / threshold / division / mass / horizon / strata / monolith / line / block / red field / blue field / dark center / worked surface / incised mark / vertical form / circular movement / edge / current / interval / passage / territory / ground / axis / veil / membrane / fracture / opening / weight / fold / layer / plane

Avoid:
InnerCore / BlueAbstract / PowerfulVortex / ModernArtwork / LuxuryAbstractPainting

### 3c. Subtitle Formula

Each subtitle must be one complete sentence.

Logic:
[visual language or artwork type] + [visible compositional characteristic] + [color, material, or atmosphere]

Requirements:

* 14 to 26 words.
* Specific to the visible artwork.
* No list of adjectives.
* No repeated sentence structure across the 3 options.

### 3d. Description Formula

For each title option, write exactly one description.

Requirements:

* 65 to 85 words.
* Exactly 5 sentences.
* Sentence 1 — Name the dominant visible form or structure and where it sits.
* Sentence 2 — Describe how the composition is organized.
* Sentence 3 — Describe color, texture, surface, or visible technique.
* Sentence 4 — Describe the resulting atmosphere from visible elements only.
* Sentence 5 — Close with scale, weight, structural tension, or material presence. Stay inside the artwork.

### 3e. Description Tone

Write like a curator who has spent real time in front of this specific artwork.

Visible fact is the foundation.
Poetic restraint is the finish.

Favor concrete verbs:
holds / cuts / rests / presses / opens / closes / divides / anchors / gathers / settles / rises / recedes

Avoid generic phrases:
evokes a contemplative atmosphere / invites the viewer / rewards a slower look / emotional dialogue / powerful visual order / compelling composition / sophisticated presence / unique artwork / creates a sense of depth / dynamic visual energy / masterful use of / timeless quality / thought-provoking

Replace generic phrases with visible facts.

## SECTION 4F — HUMAN SCALE LOCK / MANDATORY

The human figure must make the artwork's real physical size auditable.

The figure must stand on the same floor plane as the artwork and at a comparable depth from the camera.

Do not place the figure so far behind or so far in front of the artwork that scale comparison becomes unreliable.

The full standing figure should remain visible from head to shoes whenever possible.

Do not crop the figure in a way that prevents scale comparison.

The artwork height must remain physically proportional to the human figure.

Use these ratios:

If human_presence = "elegant standing male figure 1.80m":

artwork apparent height = 120 / 180 of the figure's full standing height.

If human_presence = "elegant standing female figure 1.55m":

artwork apparent height = 120 / 155 of the figure's full standing height.

This ratio is binding.

Camera angle and room type may change.
The artwork-to-figure ratio does not change.

Do not make the artwork equal to or taller than the person unless 120 actually justifies it.

Do not make a medium artwork look like:

* a mural
* an architectural wall panel
* a public installation panel
* a projection
* wallpaper
* a digital screen
* a physically enlarged print

The artwork may feel important through:

* lighting
* placement
* framing
* contrast
* human attention
* architecture
* atmosphere

but never through artificial enlargement.

Correct physical scale is more important than frame dominance.

If scale accuracy requires it, show more surrounding wall, floor, ceiling, furniture, or architecture instead of enlarging the artwork.

Use proposals 3 and 6 from the previous render logic as the intended scale model:
the artwork may feel large, desirable, and important, but it must remain physically believable beside the human figure.

Avoid the oversized scale logic seen in proposals 1 and 5, where the artwork reads as a monumental wall panel.

## SECTION 4Q — MOCKUP PROMPT FORMULA

Each mockup_prompt must be written as a complete image-generation prompt.

Build it in this exact order:

1. Open with the human moment.
   Describe who the person is, their posture, what they have just stopped doing, and their relationship to the artwork.

2. Describe the space.
   Name the architectural or regional identity and its visible materials.

3. Describe the wall and surrounding material environment.

4. Describe lighting and hour.

5. State the selected camera_view using its full technical specification:
   lens equivalent, camera height, vertical tilt if any, near edge, far edge, visible canvas depth, convergence direction, and vanishing point.

6. State camera_distance as "close-up view", but clarify that it is a collector-context close-up with enough surrounding wall, floor, architecture, and full human scale to preserve real physical size.

7. Confirm human figure placement:
   the figure must be full standing, on the same floor plane, at comparable camera depth, not blocking the artwork, and close enough to serve as a scale reference.

8. State the exact scale ratio:
   "The artwork height is 120 cm, so it must read as approximately 120/180 of the 1.80m male figure's full height"
   or
   "The artwork height is 120 cm, so it must read as approximately 120/155 of the 1.55m female figure's full height."

Use the correct ratio according to human_presence.

9. State that the artwork remains curatorially important through framing, lighting, architecture, and human attention, not through physical enlargement.

10. Preserve the artwork exactly.

## SECTION 4R — MANDATORY PRESERVATION SENTENCES

Every wall-mounted mockup_prompt must include these four sentences verbatim:

"La obra de arte en la imagen adjunta es la obra de arte final que debe presentarse, no una imagen de inspiración, y no debe ser repintada, rediseñada, mejorada, simplificada, extendida, recortada ni reinterpretada."

"Nunca modifiques la obra de arte en la imagen adjunta. Preserva la obra de arte exactamente como fue proporcionada, incluyendo composición, proporciones, orientación, estructura interna, bordes, colores, textura, pincelada, marcas, símbolos y contenido visual."

"La obra de arte debe permanecer completamente visible, correctamente proporcionada, montada naturalmente en la pared y curatorialmente dominante, sin aumentar su escala física real."

"Solo el entorno circundante, la figura humana, la iluminación, la pared, la arquitectura, el mobiliario y la atmósfera pueden ser generados o modificados."

For atelier proposals where the artwork is on an easel or leaning against a wall, use the same first, second, and fourth sentences, but replace the third sentence with:

"La obra de arte debe permanecer completamente visible, correctamente proporcionada, presentada naturalmente en el caballete o apoyada contra la pared según la escena propuesta, y curatorialmente dominante, sin aumentar su escala física real."

## SECTION 4S — CAMERA ANGLE NOTES REQUIREMENT

camera_angle_notes must include:

* selected camera_view
* near artwork edge
* receding artwork edge
* vanishing point direction
* lens equivalent
* camera height
* vertical tilt if any
* human scale ratio

Example for male figure:
"three-quarter left view: left edge near and showing canvas depth, right edge receding toward a vanishing point on the right, 50mm lens, camera at artwork vertical center, no tilt. The 120 cm artwork height must read as approximately 120/180 of the 1.80m figure's full standing height."

Example for female figure:
"seven-eighths right view: right edge near and showing canvas depth, left edge receding toward a vanishing point on the left, 35mm lens, camera at artwork vertical center, no tilt. The 120 cm artwork height must read as approximately 120/155 of the 1.55m figure's full standing height."

The ratio must be explicit and auditable.

## SECTION 4T — NEGATIVE PROMPT RULE

negative_prompt is a concise technical safeguard.

Use this base negative prompt for all proposals:

cheap decor, generic stock interior, ordinary bedroom, visual noise, logos, visible text, labels, watermarks, cropped artwork, altered artwork, rotated artwork, distorted artwork, changed artwork colors, repainted artwork, reimagined artwork, artwork content warped, artwork content stretched, artwork content simplified, artwork content extended, artwork physically oversized, artwork larger than its real dimensions, artwork equal to human full height when dimensions do not justify it, artwork taller than human figure when dimensions do not justify it, artwork tiny or lost in the room, excessively distant room view, empty room without human figure, artwork alone without human scale, human figure too far from artwork to verify scale, human figure at incompatible camera depth, cropped human figure preventing scale comparison, casual clothing, sportswear, jeans, influencer pose, smiling at camera, artwork floating, artwork used as wallpaper, artwork as mural, artwork printed on glass, artwork projected on wall, artwork converted into a digital screen, fisheye distortion, ultra-wide 16mm lens, warped perspective, exaggerated theatrical distortion, symmetrical front-on artwork edges when a three-quarter, seven-eighths, low-angle seven-eighths, or doorway angle was specified

Do not include negative terms that contradict the intended camera angle.

Do not ban all wide room visibility.
The model must be allowed to show enough room, wall, floor, architecture, and human body to preserve real scale.

Do not use "extreme angle" as a negative term for seven-eighths or low-angle seven-eighths views.

For atelier proposals with the artwork on an easel or leaning, do not include negative terms that contradict this presentation.
