1 — ANALIZA PRIMERO EL PERFIL DEL ARTISTA

Artist name: Maurizio Valch
Short bio: Maurizio Valch is a visual artist and writer whose work develops through abstract territories, constructed landscapes and materially worked surfaces. His paintings often combine fields of color, incisions, structural divisions, monolithic forms, ladders, horizons and suspended elements. The work is built through visible matter, spatial tension and a restrained sense of silence.
Preferred description tone: My work is built from visible structure, surface, color and spatial tension. Descriptions should begin with what can be seen in each artwork before moving toward atmosphere or interpretation. Use clear, sober and precise language. Avoid over-explaining the work psychologically. Interpretation should remain restrained and only appear when it is supported by visible elements.
Core artistic language: Abstract, contemporary, structural and symbolic. The visual language is built through color fields, divided territories, monolithic forms, horizons, incisions, ladders, suspended elements and spatial relationships. Emphasis should remain on composition, surface, material presence, depth, silence and visual order.
Materials and process: Primarily acrylic on canvas. The process combines layers of color, palette knife work, brushwork, veils, scraping, reduction and visible surface construction. The painting is built as a territory: adding matter, organizing planes, removing visual noise and allowing the essential structure to emerge.
Recurring symbols and motifs: Territory, silence, monoliths, horizons, tectonic lines, incisions, subtle stairs, divided fields, suspended forms, red structures, thresholds, distance, presence and the relationship between figure, space and surface. Use these motifs only when they are visibly present in the artwork.
Emotional atmosphere: Silent, sober, restrained, contemplative and slightly melancholic. The atmosphere should be read from visible color, structure, surface and space. Avoid turning every artwork into a psychological or mystical reading unless the image clearly supports it.
Target audience: Contemporary art collectors, galleries, architects, interior designers, curators and viewers interested in abstract, structural, symbolic and spatial painting. The work should be presented as serious visual art with material presence and atmospheric depth, not as decorative wall art.
Preferred mockup contexts: Refined contemporary galleries, museum-like rooms, private collections, townhouses, quiet high-end residences, minimalist interiors, architectural studios, boutique hotels, Mediterranean spaces, sober design houses, spacious salons and rooms where the artwork can breathe with silence and presence.
Excluded contexts: Generic bedrooms, kitchens, cheap decoration interiors, cluttered rooms, cold corporate offices, banal wall decor spaces, overly ornate luxury, childish interiors, rooms with too many objects, and any context that reduces the artwork to simple decoration.
Forbidden language: Avoid academic, promotional, mystical or inflated language. Do not overuse words such as profound, spiritual, mythical, psychological, transcendent, powerful, compelling, captivating, masterful, luxury, perfect for, investment, museum-quality or gallery-quality. Descriptions must remain grounded in visible structure, color, surface, material presence and spatial atmosphere.
Conceptual keywords: territory, structure, surface, silence, threshold, horizon, matter, distance, presence, construction, erosion, depth, visual order, spatial tension
Tone of voice: Sober, precise, visual, tactile, human and restrained. Describe first, interpret later. The language should feel written by someone who has carefully looked at the artwork, not by someone trying to sell or over-explain it.

REGLA DE SELECCIÓN DE VISTA DE CÁMARA

La obra de arte siempre debe permanecer correctamente orientada, proporcionada, completamente visible y visualmente dominante.

La vista de cámara seleccionada define únicamente el ángulo de cámara en relación con la obra de arte, la pared, la habitación, la iluminación, el mobiliario y la posible figura humana.

Cuando se usen términos como "front view", "three-quarter left view" o "three-quarter right view", siempre se refieren al ángulo de cámara desde el cual se fotografía la escena del mockup.

No se refieren a la posición de la obra de arte dentro de la imagen.
No significan que la obra de arte deba moverse a la izquierda, moverse a la derecha, rotarse, inclinarse, distorsionarse, angularse, recortarse ni colocarse en diagonal.

Cada propuesta contextual debe definir una vista de cámara usando solo uno de estos valores:

"front view"
"three-quarter left view"
"three-quarter right view"

REGLA DE ALTERNANCIA DE VISTA DE CÁMARA

Al generar múltiples propuestas contextuales, distribuye las vistas de cámara de la forma más equilibrada posible.

Para 3 contextos:

1 front view
1 three-quarter left view
1 three-quarter right view

Para 5 contextos:

Para 10 contextos:

No coloques la misma vista de cámara en propuestas contextuales consecutivas.

Si la obra de arte tiene textura fuerte, profundidad visible, bordes de lienzo visibles o se beneficia del contexto arquitectónico, usa más vistas de tres cuartos.

REGLA DE DISTANCIA DE CÁMARA

Cada propuesta contextual debe definir la distancia de cámara usando solo este valor:

"close-up view"

Usa "close-up view" como distancia predeterminada para todos los mockups.

Aquí, "close-up view" significa que la obra de arte debe dominar la imagen mientras todavía permite suficiente espacio circundante para comunicar calidad de pared, iluminación, presencia material de lujo, atmósfera emocional y —cuando se seleccione— escala humana.

El mockup debe sentirse como una imagen de producto de obra de arte de alta calidad colocada en un interior premium real.
No debe sentirse como una fotografía de diseño de interiores donde la habitación se vuelve más importante que la obra de arte.

Incluye solo el espacio circundante suficiente para entender la relación entre pared, iluminación, calidad material, presencia humana y atmósfera emocional.
