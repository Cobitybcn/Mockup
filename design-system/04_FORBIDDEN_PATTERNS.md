# Forbidden Patterns

## Purpose

The following patterns conflict with the established identity of Artwork Mockups. They are prohibited unless the visual system is formally revised through approved references and corresponding documentation.

## Product Identity

- Do not use Material Design.
- Do not make the application look like Bootstrap or another generic interface framework.
- Do not use Bootstrap Admin patterns.
- Do not introduce SaaS dashboards.
- Do not introduce KPI dashboards.
- Do not turn screens into administrative panels.
- Do not convert the application into a corporate productivity tool.
- Do not redesign an existing screen when the task is to extend or maintain it.
- Do not treat a new feature as permission to create a new visual direction.

## Images and Composition

- Do not reduce thumbnails until they behave like icons or dense records.
- Do not replace image-led catalogs and boards with text-first lists.
- Do not move image-specific actions away from the images they affect.
- Do not place image actions below images.
- Do not hide the artwork behind permanent controls, labels, or metadata.
- Do not fill useful white space merely to increase information density.
- Do not abandon the established vertical workflow or its horizontal galleries without an approved reference.

## Components

- Do not replace Decision Blocks with traditional SaaS buttons or generic cards.
- Do not interchange square Decision Blocks and rectangular Thumbnail Cards.
- Do not create a new component when an equivalent established pattern exists.
- Do not add large card headers and footers around image content.
- Do not create cards with excessive information.
- Do not introduce oversized toolbars.
- Do not introduce giant forms.
- Do not place repeated image actions in a global command bar.
- Do not duplicate controls.
- Do not add separate drag handles when the established thumbnail is the direct manipulation surface.
- Do not use counters as dashboard metrics.

## Color and Effects

- Do not use saturated colors as the default interface palette.
- Do not use decorative gradients.
- Do not use exaggerated glassmorphism.
- Do not use strong shadows or dramatic elevation.
- Do not use glow, neon, or high-contrast effects as decoration.
- Do not assign new colors without an established section, status, or organizational meaning.
- Do not let interface color compete with the artwork.

## Typography and Content

- Do not replace the editorial title character with corporate dashboard typography.
- Do not increase the amount of text unnecessarily.
- Do not repeat metadata that is already clear from context.
- Do not use long button labels where an established, accessible icon action already exists.
- Do not use unexplained icons or typographic glyphs as substitutes for clear iconography.
- Do not reduce primary reading and writing text to an uncomfortable scale.

## Interaction

- Do not replace a clear direct-manipulation workflow with select-then-configure steps.
- Do not make drag-and-drop destinations invisible until dragging begins.
- Do not use automatic carousel movement that interrupts visual inspection.
- Do not require hover as the only way to discover essential state or actions.
- Do not remove an accessible alternative to drag and drop.
- Do not move the user to a separate report when the result can be confirmed in the active workspace.

## Process

- Do not invent components that are not present in the product or supported by an approved reference.
- Do not add speculative design rules to this documentation.
- Do not ignore the closest visual reference.
- Do not reinterpret, modernize, or simplify an approved reference into a different visual language.
- Do not introduce implementation-specific CSS rules into the conceptual visual documentation.
- Do not create a new visual language. Always extend the existing one.

## Review Test

Reject or revise a proposal when it gives more presence to navigation, controls, metrics, effects, or explanatory text than to the artwork and the active production workspace.
