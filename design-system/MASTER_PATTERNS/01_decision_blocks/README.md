# Master Pattern: Decision Blocks

## Pattern

Decision Blocks represent consequential choices. They are used for important decisions, principal navigation, creation, changes of context, and category selection. Existing uses include Create Art, New Camera, Explore Scenes, New Series, Add Sequence, and Series.

Square geometry communicates decisions. This meaning is more important than the shape alone.

## Invariants

- Use square or strongly square-led geometry.
- Give the decision substantial presence and generous surrounding space.
- Use the established pigmentary pastel identity of its section.
- Keep the label short and direct.
- Decision Block labels must remain fully contained, optically centered, and limited to a maximum of two balanced lines.
- Keep the surface visually simple and distinct from content cards.

## Never

- Use a Decision Block to display visual content.
- Replace it with a traditional SaaS button or generic card.
- Add dense metadata, long explanations, or secondary utilities.
- Use rectangular Thumbnail Card geometry for a principal decision.
- Multiply primary decisions without a clear hierarchy.
