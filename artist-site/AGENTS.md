\# AGENTS.md — Maurizio Valch Website



This is the official website for Maurizio Valch, a contemporary abstract painter.



\## Strategic map



The website is the central authority hub.



Website = artistic identity, catalogue, SEO, collector trust, direct inquiries, and future direct sales.



Pinterest = main visual traffic source, discovery engine, and aesthetic distribution channel.



Saatchi Art = external validation and algorithmic signal. It is not the main sales channel. Links to Saatchi Art must be secondary and discreet.



Instagram, Facebook, and X = secondary social presence and distribution channels.



Do not mention or integrate Catawiki or Etsy in this website.



\## Main goal



Build a sober, premium, international website that presents Maurizio Valch as a coherent contemporary abstract artist with collectible original works.



The website must connect the artist’s own conceptual language with how real buyers search for art.



The website must support high-value original artworks and direct inquiries from collectors, interior designers, architects, and international buyers.

\## Audience



The website is aimed at:



\* art collectors

\* buyers of contemporary abstract painting

\* interior designers

\* architects

\* decorators of high-end properties

\* international buyers in the USA and Europe

\* people looking for coherent, original, collectible artwork



The audience is not only looking for an image. They are looking for coherence, trust, visual authority, artistic direction, and the possibility that the artist is building something valuable over time.



\## Art buying psychology



Buying original art online is not a purely rational process.



It is emotional, perceptual, intuitive, and trust-based.



The website must not only provide information. It must create the right conditions for desire, confidence, and recognition.



A potential collector should feel:



\* the work has presence

\* the artist has coherence

\* the paintings belong to a serious body of work

\* the website is trustworthy

\* the artwork can hold value over time

\* the inquiry process feels private, direct, and respectful

\* the work belongs in a meaningful space, not in a generic decoration catalogue



Design, copy, image rhythm, artwork detail pages, and calls to action must support this emotional and perceptual process.



Avoid making the buying journey feel mechanical, transactional, cheap, rushed, or overly rational.



The website should help the buyer move from visual attraction to trust, and from trust to inquiry.



\## Website priorities



Prioritize:



1\. Clear artistic identity.

2\. Strong visual presentation.

3\. Catalogue structure.

4\. Artwork detail pages.

5\. SEO for abstract painting and collectible original artwork.

6\. Trust for collectors.

7\. Direct inquiries from the website.

8\. Pinterest-ready pages and images.

9\. Secondary Saatchi Art links only where strategically useful.

10\. Emotional, perceptual, and trust-based buying experience.



The website must not feel like a generic portfolio, a marketplace, or a cheap ecommerce store.



\## Premium perception rule



The website must support the perception of high-value original artworks.

\* coherence

\## CTA hierarchy



Primary CTAs must favor direct contact:



\* Inquire about this work

\* Request availability

\* Contact the studio

\* Ask about this painting



Saatchi Art CTAs must be secondary and discreet:



\* View external listing on Saatchi Art

\* Also listed on Saatchi Art

\* View on Saatchi Art



Never make “Buy on Saatchi Art” the main CTA.



\## Saatchi Art rule



The reason to link to Saatchi Art is not to send away buyers as the main sales strategy.



The reason is to generate external traffic signals toward the artist’s Saatchi Art profile and works, so Saatchi Art may detect interest and potentially improve internal visibility.



The website must remain the main authority and preferred point of inquiry.



Saatchi Art should support validation, not replace the website.



\## Pinterest rule



Pinterest should be treated as the main visual traffic engine.



Website pages should be prepared to receive Pinterest traffic, especially:



\* Home

\* Works

\* Artwork detail pages

\* Series pages

\* Studio Notes

\* Collector / Inquiry pages



Use images, mockups, artwork details, and catalogue pages in a way that supports Pinterest distribution.



The website must be visually strong enough that traffic from Pinterest can convert into trust and inquiry.



\## Traffic strategy



The website is the main destination.



Pinterest should send most traffic to the website, especially to Works, artwork detail pages, Series, Studio Notes, and Collectors / Inquiries.



Saatchi Art may receive selected traffic, but only as a secondary strategic signal.



The main commercial path should be:



Pinterest → Website → Artwork Detail → Inquiry



The secondary signal path may be:



Pinterest → Saatchi Art



The website must not push interested buyers away too early.
