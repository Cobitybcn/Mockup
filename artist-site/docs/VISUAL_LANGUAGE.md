# Artist Website Visual Constitution

## Purpose

This document protects the visual language already implemented by the public Artist Website. It is evidence-based: the current templates and `assets/css/styles.css` remain the authority. New content, commerce, and tenant capabilities must extend this language without redesigning it.

## Product Character

The Artist Website is an editorial portfolio and gallery. It is not the Artwork Mockups production studio, a store theme, a marketplace, or an administrative interface.

The artwork is the dominant visual element. Navigation, information, inquiry, and commerce remain subordinate to the work.

## Protected Navigation Order

The public editorial order is:

```text
Artworks · Series · Constellations · Studio Notes · Artist · Inquire
```

Commerce utilities such as Cart must not interrupt or reorder this editorial sequence. They may be added only as quiet utilities supported by the existing header character.

## Palette

The implemented palette is warm and restrained:

- warm paper for the page;
- dark ink for primary text and commitment actions;
- pale panel surfaces for image fallbacks and grouped content;
- fine warm-gray borders;
- restrained red for editorial emphasis and active navigation;
- existing blue, ochre, and green only when their current semantic role applies.

New saturated commerce colors, decorative gradients, neon effects, and marketplace badges are not permitted.

## Typography

- Editorial serif typography carries the artist identity, artwork titles, series titles, and major headings.
- A quiet sans-serif carries navigation, specifications, controls, labels, and operational information.
- Hierarchy comes from scale, placement, and white space rather than many weights or decorative treatments.
- Reading text must remain comfortable and line lengths controlled.

## Composition

- The existing editorial maximum width and generous outer margins remain stable.
- Artwork cards remain image-led with their established proportions.
- Series retain their established grid and card composition.
- Artwork detail pages retain the large image and editorial information relationship.
- Thin borders structure content; heavy elevation does not.
- Responsive behavior preserves image scale and moves multi-column compositions into one readable column.

## Images

Images are never reduced to file icons. Existing crops, focal framing, aspect ratios, and object-fit behavior are protected unless an individual artwork requires an approved correction.

## Actions and Commerce

- Public commitment actions reuse the existing dark button treatment.
- Price, stock, print format, and cart information remain visually secondary.
- A print selector extends the artwork detail; it does not replace the editorial content.
- Cart and checkout use paper surfaces, fine borders, comfortable text, and restrained controls.
- Sold-out and unavailable states use text and the existing muted palette, not promotional badges.

## Motion

Motion remains restrained, user-initiated, and compatible with reduced-motion preferences. Commerce must not introduce automatic carousels, urgency animation, countdowns, or attention effects.

## Protected Surfaces

The following are protected until a separately approved visual change is requested:

- public header and brand relationship;
- public navigation order and active underline treatment;
- page and section spacing character;
- artwork and series card silhouettes;
- artwork detail composition;
- public button family;
- public footer character;
- responsive image priority.

## Change Test

Reject a public-site change when it makes navigation, sales controls, price, badges, or checkout more visually important than the artwork and its editorial context.
